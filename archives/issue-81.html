Hey,
Happy new year to you! Most of us started 2017 with new year resolutions…that we’ll never achieve. I’ve fell in this trap too many times myself.
   What worked for me, in retrospect, was setting small and measurable goals, and then iterating on them. Things I can do in max a week time and that are binarily either done or not (fix bug X, add feature Y). The trick is that put all together, these seemingly small and almost risible steps, in a year time, actually amount to more than what you aimed at with your new year resolutions.
Think big, start small and iterate fast.

PROTIP OF THE WEEK
 

Python: How to print the full traceback without exiting the program?

The exception handling block except Exception as ex: print(ex) will only print the exception message and not its traceback.

That’s good to know, but we need more info than this to debug properly. Namely the line that raised the exception, together with its stack trace.

The traceback module, part of the stdlib, will help us with this: 

import traceback

try:
    raise Exception(“nop”)
except Exception as ex:
    traceback.print_exc()

# Output:
Traceback (most recent call last):
  File “<stdin>”, line 2, in <module>
Exception: nop

The traceback module also works well with Python 2.x.
